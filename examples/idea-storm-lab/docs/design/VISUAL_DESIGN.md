## Reference Analysis Summary

- Borrowable traits: warm off-white surfaces, editorial rhythm, clear writing areas, compact navigation, calm accent usage, visible draft states, and content-first hierarchy.
- Do not copy: any third-party brand layout, logo, proprietary iconography, or decorative hero composition.

## Approved Palette

### User-facing hierarchy

- Primary: deep ink green for the main create/refine action and selected navigation.
- Secondary: muted clay for tags, supporting prompts, and gentle emphasis.
- Tertiary accent: soft amber for small status hints and low-priority highlights.

## Design Exclusions

- Do not make it feel like a generic SaaS admin dashboard.
- Do not use large blue-purple AI gradients.
- Do not stack decorative glass cards.
- Do not create a marketing hero as the primary experience.
- Do not over-round every element or spread content too far apart.
- Do not hide the user's current writing task behind illustration.

## Typography

Use a clean system sans-serif. Keep headings modest inside the app surface; reserve larger type for the product title and primary working context.

## Spacing and Density

Use compact but breathable spacing. Lists and editor panels should support scanning and repeated editing without becoming sparse.

## Component Language

- Buttons are quiet and task-focused.
- Cards are only used for repeated items or framed tools, not nested page sections.
- Inputs and text areas should feel like writing surfaces, with clear focus states.
- Tags use the secondary color sparingly.

## Layout Principles

- Prioritize the current idea, draft, or feedback queue above decoration.
- Keep navigation, list, and editing areas visible on desktop.
- Collapse to a single-column flow on mobile without horizontal scrolling.

## Interaction and Motion Notes

Motion should be subtle and functional: focus, selection, save state, and loading feedback. Avoid decorative animation loops.

## Accessibility Notes

Maintain visible focus rings, readable contrast for muted text, and stable layout when content wraps.
